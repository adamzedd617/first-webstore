How to create account

  1. When you landed on main page of our EzTopup website, on top right of your browser, you can see the user icon.

  2. Click the icon and there will be Log In and Register menu.

  3. If you already has EzTopup account, click the Log in menu if not click the Register menu.

  4. Fill in all the required information needed to create your account.

  5. Once submit, you can log in to EzTopup using the account you just created.

Why are your account are suspended

There are many reason as to why you could be ban.

  1. Share/Hijack Account
    Sharing personal EzTopup information in order to obtain access to another person's account on EzTopup. Changing another person's email address, and/or password without their permission in order to gain and control access to an account. Offender attempted to use or take content they did not pay for.

  2. Another Person's Personal Information.
    Offender revealed personal information about someone else in a public area, such as email addresses, phone numbers, or physical addresses.

  3. Impersonating Another User.
    Offender created an account with close similarity to an existing user, with perceived intentions to impersonate the other user without their consent.

Basic & Safety Tips

  1. Keep a clean machine
    Keep all software on internet-connected devices – including PCs, smartphones and tablets – up to date to reduce risk of infection from malware.

  2. Lock down your login
    Fortify your online accounts by enabling the strongest authentication tools available, such as biometrics, security keys or a unique one-time code through an app on your mobile device. Your usernames and passwords are not enough to protect key accounts like email, banking and social media.

  3. Make your password a sentence
    A strong password is a sentence that is at least 12 characters long. Focus on positive sentences or phrases that you like to think about and are easy to remember (for example, “I love country music.”). On many sites, you can even use spaces!

  4. Unique account, unique password
    Having separate passwords for every account helps to thwart cybercriminals. At a minimum, separate your work and personal accounts and make sure that your critical accounts have the strongest passwords.
